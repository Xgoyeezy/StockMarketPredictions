## Product Definition

Quant Evidence OS is a trading evidence and research operating system. It scans equities and ETFs, runs paper-first strategy desks, ranks paper candidates, explains trade and no-trade decisions, tracks blockers and missed opportunities, validates forecasts, evaluates execution quality, and turns decisions into reusable evidence.

Current strategy desks:

- Macro Trend Desk.
- Stat Arb Desk.
- Equities Momentum Desk.
- Event-Driven Desk.
- Options Volatility Desk.

Current positioning should treat the system as a paper-first evidence and research workflow, not a guaranteed profit system, AI trading bot, HFT platform, live autonomous money manager, broker replacement, investment adviser, black-box alpha machine, compliance-approved product, or proven alpha system.

Future roadmap language should keep the same separation. Market Specialist Desks are context engines, not order bots. Strategy Desks produce trade logic. Candidate Fusion may combine market context and strategy logic in a future project, while risk gates stay above both. The Visual Strategy Evidence Builder is a no-code evidence contract builder, not a no-code trading bot. The Off-Exchange Liquidity Dashboard is passive research context, not a trade trigger. Broker-neutral execution planning means Alpaca becomes one adapter, not that Quant Evidence OS becomes a broker. C++ Core Accelerators are performance helpers only and must not own trading authority.

## Best First Buyer

The best first serious buyer is a serious solo systematic trader or advanced retail trader who wants proof, paper automation, forecast validation, execution quality, benchmark comparison, and decision review.

Why:

- They can tolerate paper-first operation.
- They care about why trades did or did not happen.
- They benefit from missed move reports and blocker analytics.
- They can understand reward, forecast, baseline, and no-trade evidence.
- They do not require full firm governance on day one.

The second buyer is a small prop research team or small fund researcher evaluating an evidence workflow. That buyer will require more governance, RBAC, model registry, portfolio risk, and audit controls before serious adoption.

## Buyer Profile

Best-fit early users are serious self-directed traders who already think in terms of paper validation, repeatability, costs, missed opportunities, forecast quality, and decision review. They should want to know what the system saw, what it blocked, what it missed, what it predicted, what happened afterward, and what evidence should change next after manual review.

Poor-fit early users are buyers looking for guaranteed returns, live autonomous money management, HFT execution, broker custody, regulatory advice, or a black-box alpha machine.

## Best Next Proof Milestone

The best next proof milestone is Professional Benchmark plus Walk-Forward results. Until that proves repeatable edge after costs, Quant Evidence OS should be described as a strong evidence-control platform, not a proven alpha machine.

## Why HFT Is Not The First Target

HFT or elite execution is the weakest current category at 2/10. Quant Evidence OS is Alpaca-paper-first and evidence-oriented. It is not direct-market-access infrastructure, does not have colocation, does not reconstruct full order books, does not model queue position, and does not operate with nanosecond or microsecond execution controls.

Do not target HFT first. Focus on research, evidence, forecast validation, risk, benchmark proof, walk-forward validation, and execution quality for intraday and swing timeframes.

## Free-First And Provider ROI Positioning

There is no truly free way to trade every market. Position the provider strategy as free-first and proof-gated:

1. Free simulator.
2. Alpaca paper.
3. ETF proxies.
4. Free or delayed data.
5. Provider abstraction.
6. Paid services only when evidence proves the need.

Do not recommend paid providers because the product feels advanced. Pay only when Data Completeness, benchmark, execution quality, or walk-forward evidence proves a specific bottleneck and the expected monthly value is at least 3x monthly cost. Prefer 5x before depending on a paid service.

Acceptable free-first examples:

- Alpaca paper for U.S. stocks, ETFs, options, and supported paper workflows.
- BrokerSimulatorAdapter for future markets.
- ETF proxies for gold, silver, oil, rates, FX, indexes, sectors, and volatility context.
- Crypto data-only first.
- Futures research-only through ETFs first.
- Off-exchange analytics through free delayed research data where available.

ETF proxy examples include GLD, IAU, GDX, SLV, USO, XLE, OIH, TLT, IEF, SHY, BIL, UUP, FXE, FXY, FXB, SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLY, XLP, XLI, XLU, XLB, XLRE, and BTC or ETH ETFs where available.
