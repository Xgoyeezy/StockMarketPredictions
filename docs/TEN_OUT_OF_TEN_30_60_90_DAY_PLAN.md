# Ten Out Of Ten 30-60-90 Day Plan

This plan sequences the work needed to move Quant Evidence OS categories toward 10/10 readiness. It is planning only and does not implement roadmap features, add services, add routes, add pages, modify execution, modify broker routes, modify live-control behavior, modify risk gates, enable live trading, clear kill switches, grant AI order authority, or let analytics change ranking weights automatically.

Ratings are current estimated readiness scores. They are not proof of alpha and are not investor performance claims. Benchmark proof is required before claiming edge. Walk-forward proof is required before claiming repeatability. Paper-first safety remains the active execution boundary. Reward and forecast analytics are research-only. AI has no order authority. Risk gates remain authoritative. Broker routes remain unchanged. Live-money autonomy is not enabled. Promotion status is research metadata unless separately approved by a future explicit governance framework.

Proof-first roadmap discipline is defined in `docs/PROOF_FIRST_ROADMAP_DISCIPLINE.md`. Ambition is allowed, but proof decides priority. This 30-60-90 plan must protect the current foundation before any expansion layer moves into active work.

## Current Category Baseline

| Category | Current estimated readiness | Main 90-day target |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Retail trading bot | 9/10 | Move toward 10/10 usability after proof and safety are clear. |
| Solo systematic trader platform | 7.5/10 | Move toward 9/10 through benchmark, walk-forward, data, score, and execution proof. |
| Small prop shop or small fund research stack | 6/10 | Move toward 7/10 through research promotion, portfolio risk, and governance planning. |
| Top discretionary trader comparison | 5/10 | Move toward 6/10 through shadow-mode maturity. |
| Institutional quant desk or enterprise control plane | 3/10 | Move toward 4/10 through lineage and governance planning. |
| HFT or elite execution platform | 2/10 | Stay 2/10 unless separate future feasibility study is approved. |

## Rating Movement Rules

- The expected rating movement in this document is directional planning, not a rating update.
- Actual rating changes require the matching checklist items in `docs/TEN_OUT_OF_TEN_ACCEPTANCE_CHECKLIST.md` and proof gates in `docs/TEN_OUT_OF_TEN_PROOF_GATES.md`.
- If benchmark or walk-forward proof is missing, keep Solo Systematic below 10/10.
- If RBAC, approvals, audit, registries, and portfolio risk are missing, keep Small Fund below 10/10.
- If same-opportunity human-vs-system proof is missing, keep Discretionary Comparison below 10/10.
- If lineage, permissions, environment separation, incident handling, firm-grade reporting, and external review are missing, keep Institutional below 10/10.
- If direct-market-access infrastructure and a separate latency thesis are missing, keep HFT at future-only status.

## 30 Day Plan

- Safety verification.
- Data Completeness.
- Professional Benchmark.
- Walk-Forward basics.
- Score Calibration.
- Execution Quality cleanup.

- Safety audit checklist and verification report.
- Data completeness threshold definitions.
- Benchmark methodology hardening plan.
- Walk-forward frozen snapshot requirements.
- Score bucket separation and feature attribution requirements.
- Execution cost field and TCA maturity plan.

What to verify:

- No execution behavior changed.
- No live trading enabled.
- No broker route logic changed.
- No order logic changed.
- No risk gate logic changed.
- No AI order authority added.
- No ranking weights changed automatically.
- Simulation evidence remains separate from real-time market-observed evidence.

What to measure:

- Data completeness rate.
- Rewardability rate.
- Missing forward returns.
- Missing baselines.
- Missing forecast actuals.
- Missing slippage/spread/fill delay.
- Missing regime labels.
- Benchmarkable record count.

What to avoid:

- New broker routes.
- Live trading flows.
- Automatic promotion.
- Automatic ranking-weight changes.
- HFT implementation.
- Institutional-grade claims.

Categories improved:

- Retail: clearer paper-only safety and no-trade proof.
- Solo systematic: strongest improvement through proof chain.
- Small fund: foundation for promotion review.
- Discretionary: foundation for same-opportunity comparison.
- Institutional: foundation for data and audit lineage.
- HFT: no upgrade targeted.

Expected rating movement:

- Retail: 9/10 stays 9/10 until onboarding and demo mode are complete.
- Solo systematic: 7.5/10 toward 8/10 if benchmarkable data and first frozen walk-forward design are ready.
- Small fund: 6/10 toward 6.5/10 if research promotion evidence links are clarified.
- Discretionary: 5/10 stays 5/10 unless shadow contracts are defined.
- Institutional: 3/10 toward 3.5/10 if lineage gaps are documented.
- HFT: 2/10 unchanged.

Risks:

- Benchmark work exposes insufficient data quality.
- Score calibration recommendations are misread as automatic tuning.

Acceptance criteria:

- Data Completeness identifies blocker fields for benchmark readiness.
- Professional Benchmark has clear pass/fail proof criteria.
- Walk-forward snapshot requirements are documented.
- Safety boundaries are unchanged.

## Replanning Triggers

Replan the 30-60-90 sequence if any of these occur:

- Safety verification finds live autonomy, broker-route mutation, order mutation, risk-gate bypass, kill-switch bypass, AI order authority, or automatic ranking-weight changes.
- Data Completeness shows benchmark-critical fields below the configured threshold.
- Professional Benchmark shows no edge or only pre-cost edge.
- Walk-Forward fails or has too small a frozen out-of-sample sample.
- Execution Quality shows costs erase the apparent edge.
- Support or firm-review exports expose secrets, broker records, raw logs, account IDs, raw local paths, or credentials.
- Any stakeholder wants HFT work before the evidence/research proof chain is complete.

## Highest Priority Build

The highest priority build is the proof chain for the solo systematic trader category. Use this proof-first near-term order:

1. Post-Implementation Verification
2. Data Completeness cleanup
3. Professional Benchmark hardening

6. Execution Quality and TCA
7. Risk Gate and Audit Trail hardening

This should happen before governance scale-up, institutional positioning, or any HFT feasibility work.
